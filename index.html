The Framework

What is DiSC?

DiSC is a behavior-based model that identifies four primary communication and personality styles. Understanding these styles — in yourself and in others — transforms how you listen, respond, and connect.

DDominance

Driven, direct, and decisive. D-types communicate with confidence and are focused on results and forward momentum.

The Approach

How I teach it

Learning DiSC isn’t about putting people in boxes — it’s about building the awareness to meet people where they are.

  • 01

    Know your own style first

    Before you can adapt to others, you need a clear picture of your own default tendencies — how you send messages, how you process conflict, and where your blind spots are.

  • 02

    Read the person in front of you

    DiSC gives you a vocabulary for observing behavior in real time. You learn to pick up on signals — pace, priority, tone — and adjust before friction builds.

  • 03

    Adapt without losing yourself

    Adapting your style is a skill, not a performance. I teach techniques that feel natural — so you can flex toward any style while staying grounded in who you are.

  • 04

    Apply it to real conversations

    Teams, one-on-ones, difficult conversations, leadership moments — DiSC becomes most powerful when it’s tied to the actual situations you face every day.

What changes

The shift people experience.

DiSC doesn’t change who you are — it changes how much of yourself lands with other people.

You stop taking friction personally.

When you understand that someone’s bluntness is their default, not a slight, the emotional charge drains out of difficult interactions.

You know how to reach anyone.

Results-first or relationship-first, thorough or fast-moving — you have a concrete approach for every type, not just the ones who think like you do.

Hard conversations get easier.

When you understand someone’s style, you stop guessing at how to frame things. You know what they need to hear before they can actually hear it.

Teams stop talking past each other.

A shared DiSC vocabulary turns “I don’t know why we can’t get aligned” into a diagnosable, solvable pattern — not a personality flaw.

FAQ

Common questions.

  • DiSC is a behavior-based model that describes four primary communication and work styles: Dominance, Influence, Steadiness, and Conscientiousness. Unlike Myers-Briggs, which focuses on how you think and process information internally, DiSC focuses specifically on how you communicate, what you prioritize in interactions, and how you respond to others. It’s less about identity and more about behavior — which makes it much more actionable in real conversations.
  • No — though it helps. A formal assessment gives you a detailed profile report and adds precision to the work. But it’s not a prerequisite. Many people can identify their dominant style through a conversation, and a lot of useful work happens without ever taking a formal test. If you’d like to use an official assessment, we can discuss options as part of the engagement.
  • The standard format is a half-day (3–4 hours), which covers the core framework, individual profiles, and team application exercises. A full-day version goes deeper into cross-style communication, conflict patterns, and leadership dynamics. I can also run condensed 90-minute versions for teams with limited time — reach out and we can figure out what makes sense for your situation.
  • Both. The workshop is designed for teams, but 1-on-1 coaching is for individuals who want to work through their own communication patterns — whether it’s navigating a specific relationship at work, preparing for a high-stakes conversation, or just understanding why certain interactions keep going sideways.
  • That depends entirely on what you do with it. Understanding your style is the first step — most people have a “that’s exactly me” moment pretty quickly. The real shift happens when you start applying it: noticing someone else’s style in a live conversation, adjusting how you frame something, and seeing it actually land differently. That takes practice, which is what the exercises and coaching are for. DiSC is a tool, not a personality test you take once and forget.
